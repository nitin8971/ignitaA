Common Questions

Everything you want to know

Honest answers — no fluff.

Is IgnitaA telling me to change my career?
+

Never. IgnitaA is a self-discovery platform, not a career service. We help you explore what lights you up. What you do with that discovery is entirely yours — keep it a beloved hobby, build something from it on the side, or eventually go deeper. We provide the space and tools; you make every decision.

What if I don't know what my passion is?
+

Discovery Mode was built exactly for this. Over 30 days you try small activities across different passion worlds. The platform tracks which ones you naturally return to. Many users — including students and people between life chapters — have found their passion category through this mode, often something they'd done casually for years.

What are Spark Challenges?
+

Each week, every passion community runs a challenge: "Best 60-second guitar cover," "Most creative food photo," "Best haiku this week." The community votes. Winners are featured on the Recognition Wall, earn a badge, and often receive a free mentor session. This is how hidden talent gets found.

What is the Collab Board?
+

A board where users post what they can offer and what they need — across passion worlds. A musician can find a filmmaker. A writer can find an illustrator. A dancer can find a photographer. These cross-passion collaborations produce the most creative and surprising work on the platform.

How does Hobby History work?
+

Before you enter your dashboard, we ask you to write a short origin story: when did you first love this, and why? You can add a photo from that time too. This becomes the most personal part of your public profile — the part people connect with most and remember longest.

Is IgnitaA only for Indians?
+

IgnitaA launched in India first but every passion world is open to anyone, anywhere. The platform is in English and communities are globally mixed. The Collab Board especially benefits from international members — a musician in Bengaluru collaborating with a videographer in Mumbai or a writer in London.
